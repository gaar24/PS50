<br> #### Week 2: 2D Cutting <h4 style="font-size:30px;background-color:lightpink; color:white; font-family: helvetica"> 1ST ASSIGNMENT</h4> For this week's assignment we had to create press fit construction kit that could be able to be assembled in different ways so that we could then send it to a laser cut. It had to be done so that the pieces could be stay together, but not glue or other material could be used to pull them together. <h4> - Brainstorming design </h4> Although I started with no design in mind, it drastically changed when took a paper and a pencil and started drawing the figure I wanted to achieve by using the lasercut. First, I thought of creating a sort of animal with different shapes, but didnt felt that much inspiration. So, i decided to try to do a pencil holder why rectangles. Now, I will be showing the different steps I had to do before reaching my goal. <h4> - Step 1 | Learn How to Use Fusion 360 </h4> Before I coul start, I needed several youtube videos and pdfs to learn how to use fusion 360 and the diverse techiques that I would need to apply to my design. For that, I started by doing a simple shape provided in an example. Once I felt confidence, I began my project. <h4> - Step 2 | Design the Model </h4> I decided to do a sort of wheel with eight notvhes of about 1.90mm. The first thing I did was to create a sketch and draw a circle in the top plane that had a diameter of 50mm. Once I had this, I created the offLines along the vertical and horizontal axes. The, I mirror them and extrude it with the thickness of the cardboard, which was the material I will be using, which was 3.8 mmm. Then saved it as a dfx and went to the lasercurt. <img width="225" height= "300" align-items="center" src="./02_cutting/bula.png" alt="brainstorming"/> <h4> - Step 3 | Cutting </h4> <h4> - Step 4 | Assembly </h4> <h4> Final Result </h4> For the final results, I think that it definetely wwent as expected, as it not only was able to assembled in multiple ways, but also fit perfectly one with each other.
